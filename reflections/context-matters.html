🔍 How Context Changes Who’s Better?

What’s the point of simplicity when you can complicate your research method and call it "thorough"? 🤔

Ibrahim vs. Fahd:

🏃‍♂️ Ibrahim ran 10 km in one hour, while Fahd took 1.5 hours. Based on time alone, Ibrahim looks like the clear winner.

But wait… what if I told you Ibrahim ran on a prepared track, while Fahd ran on rough terrain? Suddenly, Fahd’s effort seems a bit more impressive, doesn’t it? 🌍

Now let’s add another detail: Ibrahim weighs 90 kg, while Fahd weighs just 55 kg. Now, Ibrahim’s performance starts to stand out. 💪

Oh, and one more thing—Ibrahim is 23 years old, and Fahd is 74. Now Fahd’s run feels almost extraordinary! 🎉

Each new detail shifts the basis for comparison. With every additional piece of information, the context changes, and so does the judgment of who’s better. 📊

The same thing happens when an HR manager compares job candidates—or when academics like us evaluate different research methods. 🧠

Each new factor significantly changes the perceived value and effectiveness of a method, making objective assessment challenging. That’s why a comprehensive evaluation that considers all relevant factors is crucial for a fair comparison. ✅
